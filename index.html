We have an extremely balanced team. The first letter in the Meyers Brigg's Personality test is extroversion (E) or introversion (I), which measures general attitude [1]. Both James and Jamie show a preference for extroversion, and Brandon and Ryan show a preference for introversion.

The second letter stands for intuition (N) or sensing (S), which measures perception. Once again, we have two intuitive types, James and Jamie; and two sensing types, Brandon and Ryan.

The third letter is thinking (T) or feeling (F), which measures judgement, with the entire team favouring feeling.

The fourth letter is judging (J) or perceiving (P), which measures whether someone is more perceptive or more judgemental, with most of the team favouring perception.


We have a variety of learning styles; however, visual learners seem more common. Our team seems to be naturally balanced, and it shows in our team work.

Communication has been frequent and effortless.

Ideal Jobs

The vast majority of our team wants to become developers, with Brandon wanting to become a jack-of-all trades and work as a consultant.

The difference between what each developer finds to be an ideal job may lie in short VS long term thinking. A full stack developer is a developer with such a wealth of knowledge they can build an application's front and backend from scratch, and then deploy it.

Striving to be a full stack developer is a longer term goal because of the knowledge required; whereas, a junior developer has graduate-level knowledge.

All of the would-be developers on our team are primarily focussed on web development.

Brandon's ideal job as a consultant is motivated by helping people. A consultant, after all, solves IT problems. His background in design will be an invaluable asset to future clients.

Overall, our team's collective knowledge on web development and design will serve us well on any project. Likewise, th balance of our personality types is highly conducive to teamwork.

Industry Data

Exploring demographics of ideal jobs

Search Results by Keyword LinkedIn

The most in demand job from our team's ideal jobs, as confirmed by linkedIn search results, is an IT consultant, coming in at 694 results from a total of 1386 results (50%).

In second place is full stack developer, coming in at 574 results (41%).

In last place is junior developer, coming in at 118 results (8%).

This supports the idea that the demand for senior developers far exceeds the demand for junior developers; likewise, the jack-of-all trades consultant's skillset isn't limited to working in software alone, so demand is high for good reason.

This trend is mirrored in the data provided by Burning Glass Technologies in that developer ranks lower than fullstack developer, which ranks lower than senior developer, and at the very top of their chart is a job title solutions architect (Top IT Job Titles March, 2018), which is a fancy term for a consultant.

IT Work

Interviewing people

  • Interview with Sam Byrne, Web designer at Zest Launceston office.
  • 41 Cameron street
  • P.O Box2132, Launceston, Tas, 7250
  • P.03 6334 3319
  • E. Sam@zesttas.com.au

Sam is a Web designer at zest. He is the only designer and marketer in the building and works on all the client’s websites. Sam’s day to day work consists of rocking up to the office and greeting whoever is in the open workspace. He then proceeds to make his first coffee for the day, then Sam makes his way to his desk where he checks his emails to see if anything new has come through overnight. He then opens zest’s workflow files to see his current and future projects. From there Sam’s day can vary from meeting with clients to sitting down and designing all day, Sam could also push some work to Owen Richards to develop a finished design. Sam also makes multiply coffees over the span of the day he says.

Sam says, “he works in the I.T. and marketing industry and it is an everchanging environment, it’s not a boring working place as one task is never the same”. Apart from designing the websites he makes. He also has to work with accountant’s web developers and client’s needs. Apart from the clients Sam works with, his main partner in crime is a Web Developer by the name of Owen Richards. Owen runs a business called O-WEB and he is an off-site consultant for zest Launceston. Apart from Owen, Sam does not really interact with anyone from the I.T. world because Zest is a small business in Launceston, Tasmania. He only works directly with his clients he has and his web dev Owen.

Sam has most of the interaction with the clients as he is the first point of call for a website. Sam meets with the client to see what sort of website that they would like him to build. Sam spends most of his time coming up with the design of the client’s website. Sam also spends a lot of his time laying his design out and picking the most suitable style for the website. Sam’s most challenging thing is when a client sees the finished product then sees something that they might not like and wants his to change the website to please the client’s needs. He says, “you really need to get it right the first time”.
